Keep Looking at the Data

It’s a tale as old as time - everybody lies, but the numbers don’t. Eliot is a perennial contender, year after year, and by all metrics could be at the top of the leaderboard – but by the power of ethereal life forms, it just doesn’t happen. Last year was disappointing; despite top 3 individual power rankings in both Kubb and Spikeball, US v Zantac found themselves at the bottom of the totem pole in 2024. However, all hope is not lost – Eliot has found himself a familiar face to team up with this year – the one and only Nick “Lenny” Panarello. Their chemistry is undeniable; the piggyback rides, the unconditional love, and the militaristic leadership roles the team has got to be terrifying for the other teams. They’re unproven in Team competition, but everyone knows Turn Around Lenny could be an absolute force to reckon with.Three years, three subpar results. Unlike Brock Purdy, Eliot has yet to find a way to thrive in the system.

Where's my Intern?

Another familiar face with another familiar story – Tim Takach is the laboratory-built embodiment of the Big Body Games. On paper, he still looks like he’s got it all – the height, the girth, the talent; however, the sabermetrics reveal something intangible. Despite his physical gifts, he hasn’t had ‘that dog’ in him that everyone is hoping to see unlocked. Despite topping the statistics in Spikeball and Beer Die, we can call it nothing short of a full mental collapse in the other 3 games that left Boost Looters out of contention for 2024. But wait – there’s more; this is Timmy’s last game before entering the lifelong embrace of fatherhood. Could that be the linchpin that gives Tim the edge we’ve all been waiting to see? Could Baby Takach be the final inspiration that unlocks him into proving that he is the best of the best? A BBG championship could just be icing on the cake for what is shaping up to be a great year for the Takach family, but only one athlete can decide if the time is now for victory.
